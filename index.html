Welcome! Just trying to figure this out. Techies like to think everything they do is so logical and therefore the conclusions derived are self-evident. But a lot of it seems to me to be post hoc rationalisation. It works because it works, because it works, etc. The set up for this page could have been so much easier if more thought and care had been put into it. There is an underlying assumption in engineering that somehow something is self-evident just by being so. You see a lot of this in academia where it is assumed that facts are enough. All hail, to those who master them, but the value is in the connections, context, and relatedness. I think it is a good thing that AI will allow folks like myself to build and create without ever having to interface with those macho cretins at Stack Overflow and elsewhere. Being overly complicated and obscure is off-putting in the extreme and does not make one think highly of those who engage in self-promoting obfuscation. The sooner sites like that become redundant the better. Rant over. Be back soon.
